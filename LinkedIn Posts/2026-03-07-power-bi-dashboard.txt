Most companies get Power BI dashboards completely wrong. 
   
They invest in the tool, but end up with a jumbled mess of charts and tables that don't tell a story. The result? Decision-makers are still stuck in spreadsheet chaos, unable to get the insights they need.

To create a Power BI dashboard that drives real business value, you need to focus on three key areas:
→ Align your metrics with business objectives
→ Use data visualization best practices to make insights pop
→ Implement a data governance framework to ensure accuracy and security

Here's a step-by-step approach to get you started:
1. Identify your key performance indicators (KPIs) and map them to business outcomes
2. Design your dashboard with a clear hierarchy of information, using filters and slicers to enable user interaction
3. Set up a data refresh schedule to ensure your insights are always up-to-date
