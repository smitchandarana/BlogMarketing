Unpopular opinion: Most companies get data governance completely wrong. 
   
Data governance is not just about creating rules and policies; it's about creating a culture that values data as a strategic asset. 
Many organizations struggle with data silos, manual reporting, and spreadsheet chaos, which leads to poor decision-making and wasted resources.

To establish effective data governance, follow these steps:
1. **Define data ownership**: Identify who is responsible for each dataset and establish clear roles and responsibilities.
2. **Establish data standards**: Develop common data definitions, formats, and quality metrics to ensure consistency across the organization.
3. **Create a data catalog**: Build a centralized repository of all datasets, including metadata, to facilitate discovery and access.
4. **Develop a data security framework**: Implement robust security measures to protect sensitive data and prevent unauthorized access.
